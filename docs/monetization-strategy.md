# Capsule — Monetization Strategy Brief

> **Thesis:** The whole model rests on one line — *the people you invite never pay.* Charging the network's participants would strangle the exact virality that makes a social app worth building. So we monetize the **host**, and we monetize the **occasion** — twice, in two very different ways.

*Prepared for Capsule (indie) · Horizon: MVP → Scale · Evidence base: 2025–26 benchmarks.*

## The recommendation in one breath

1. **Run two revenue engines, not one.** A creator subscription (**Capsule Pro**) for recurring value, and a one-time **Event Pass** for weddings/reunions/trips. The Event Pass is the differentiated, subscription-fatigue-proof money-maker — no competitor owns that flow.
2. **Never paywall participation.** Joining, viewing, contributing, reacting, voting, and the unlock reveal stay free forever, for everyone. These are the app's oxygen, not its inventory.
3. **Price Pro at $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr** — deliberately below the ~$9.99 market mode to protect virality — and **lead with annual**. Add a launch-only **$79 Lifetime** to pull forward cash and evangelists.
4. **Skip weekly plans.** The LTV data loves them; the brand doesn't. Weekly billing signals "dating app," not "keepsake." Wrong emotional register.
5. **Show the paywall at the moment of success** — right after a capsule unlocks, or when a host hits a cap while inviting — never on a cold app open.
6. **Turn invites into a funnel.** Referral rewards (a free Pro month / bonus storage for invites that convert) make virality and revenue the same motion.

## 01 · Freemium psychology

Freemium isn't "give stuff away and hope." It's a customer-acquisition engine where the free tier is the marketing budget. It works when the free experience is *complete enough to build a habit and generate invites*, and the paid tier removes a ceiling the user only feels once they're already hooked.

**Why it succeeds**

- **The free tier is distribution.** Every free user who invites others is unpaid acquisition. For a social app, the free cohort *is* the growth loop.
- **Value precedes payment.** Users convert after they've felt the product work — a capsule unlocking with 40 photos from friends is the "aha," and the ask lands right after it.
- **Endowment & loss-aversion.** Once a user has memories or a group living inside your app, leaving means abandoning something. Paying to keep it feels cheaper than losing it.

**Why it fails — the three killers**

- **Paywalling the habit.** Gate something users need *daily* and they churn before they love the product. The free tier must be a real product, not a demo.
- **A stingy free tier that blocks virality.** If free users can't invite freely, your acquisition loop is capped at your paying users — fatal for a network-effects app.
- **A premium tier no one can articulate.** If a user can't finish the sentence "I'd pay because ___," they won't. Vague "pro" bundles convert worse than one sharp, nameable benefit.

**What actually makes users convert**

- **Hitting a wall mid-momentum** — the 4th capsule, the 11th invitee, the 21st photo. A limit felt while doing something enjoyable is the highest-converting paywall.
- **A high-stakes occasion.** A wedding or once-in-a-lifetime trip collapses price sensitivity. People who'd never pay $5/mo happily pay $25 once for *this* event.
- **Social proof & status** — "your friend has Pro," badges, a nicer-looking capsule others see.

> **Benchmark reality:** Median **freemium** free-to-paid conversion sits around **2.2%**; hard-paywall apps convert ~**12%** but forfeit the top-of-funnel a social app lives on (RevenueCat 2025). For Capsule, a **1–3% blended conversion is a healthy target** — the free base's job is reach and invites, and a growing share of revenue should come from *one-time Event Passes* that never touch the subscription funnel.

> **The pattern:** access and participation are free; friction, power, storage, status, and aesthetics are paid. Capsule should sit closest to **Notion + Tinybeans** for its subscription, borrow **Snapchat+'s cosmetic/status playbook**, and reframe **Tinder's à-la-carte idea** as the Event Pass.

## 03 · Subscription pricing

**Monthly vs. yearly — lead with annual.** Annual plans dramatically improve LTV: 12 months up front, less churn exposure, ~66% first-year renewal (RevenueCat). Present annual as the default/highlighted option with the monthly-equivalent shown ("$39.99/yr — just $3.33/mo"), which can 2–3× annual selection. Keep monthly as a low-commitment on-ramp, but don't feature it.

**Weekly plans — the data says yes, the brand says no.** Weekly subs now generate ~47% of consumer subscription revenue and, paired with a trial, produce the highest 12-month LTV (~$49/user) (Adapty/RevenueCat 2025).

> **Competing viewpoints — my call:** Weekly billing is optimized for *impulse and urgency* (utilities, dating, AI toys). Capsule is a *keepsake* — the emotional opposite. A weekly charge on a memory app reads as extractive and erodes the trust the whole brand depends on. The revenue-max answer is weekly; the brand-and-retention answer is annual-led. For a sentimental social app building for the long term, **take the brand answer.** If you ever want the weekly LTV mechanics, express them as the one-time **Event Pass** — same "pay for a burst of value" psychology, none of the recurring-charge ick.

**Free trials — 14 days on annual, and win it on Day 0.** Longer trials convert better (17–32 day ~42% vs. ~25% for sub-4-day), but the real lever is that **80–90% of trials start on Day 0** — conversion is decided by onboarding, not trial length (RevenueCat 2025). Recommended: a **14-day trial attached to the annual plan** (Headspace pattern — a longer trial lowers the psychological bar of a bigger commitment). Require a card only once you have scale; pre-scale, a no-card trial maximizes starts.

**Psychological pricing, discounts, family & student**

- **Charm pricing** ($4.99, $39.99) still beats round numbers for impulse tiers.
- **Anchor high, sell the middle.** A visible $9.99 Premium makes $4.99 Pro feel sensible (decoy effect).
- **Intro discounts:** a first-year "$29.99 (then $39.99)" annual offer converts well; use sparingly so users don't learn to wait for deals.
- **Family plan: defer.** Capsule is *already* multi-user per capsule, so a "family plan" is redundant. A future household/group subscription could work, but it's not a launch priority.
- **Student discount:** low effort, good will; Capsule skews young (reunions, trips, dorm life). A simple 50%-off code post-launch, not a Day-1 build.

**Lifetime — a launch tactic, not a forever product.** A **$79 launch Lifetime** (first 90 days only, then retired) pulls forward cash and mints committed evangelists when word-of-mouth matters most. The risk is real (lost recurring revenue, power-user skew), so **cap it, time-box it, and kill it** once subscription traction exists. Never a standing option.

## 04 · Free-vs-paid framework

Every feature sorts into one of five buckets. The discipline is deciding *why* — and the most important bucket is the last one.

**① Always free — the core loop.** Anything a user must do to get value or generate an invite: join / view / contribute / react, vote in awards, see the unlock reveal, create capsules (a real quota, not 1), invite by link & QR, add friends.

**② Nice upgrades — ceiling-raisers.** Quotas a happy user eventually bumps into; cost-justified and easy to explain: unlimited capsules (past ~3 active), video, more storage & higher member caps, ZIP export (download your whole capsule).

**③ Power-user — depth & automation.** Features only the most invested hosts want; high WTP, low reach cost: recurring groups (auto-capsules), custom voting windows / advanced awards, bulk ZIP export, proximity-unlock for big events.

**④ Luxury / convenience — cosmetic & status.** Pure margin; nobody *needs* them: premium accent palettes & capsule themes, branded title cards, premium badge, custom app icons.

**⑤ Never paywall — growth-critical.** Gating any throttles the network: being invited / joining, uploading to a capsule you're in, the unlock & reveal moment, sharing links / QR / basic notifications.

> **The one rule that governs the rest:** if a feature's job is to **bring more people in or keep the loop turning**, it's bucket ⑤ — free, always. If its job is to **remove friction, add power, or confer status for the host**, it's fair game to charge for. When in doubt: "Does gating this cost me invites?" If yes, don't.

## 05 · Feature scoring model

Score each new feature 1–5 on five axes, then read the disposition off the totals.

| Axis | Question | Low (1) | High (5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Growth impact | Does gating it reduce invites, joins, or participation? | Not at all — solo/host-only | Severely — part of the viral loop |
| Willingness-to-pay | Would a host consciously pay for this? | No — invisible/expected | Yes — nameable, "worth it" |
| Marginal cost | Does each use cost real money (storage, video, AI)? | ~Free to serve | Expensive per use |
| Frequency of need | How often does a typical user hit it? | Rare / one-off | Daily / every session |
| Substitutability | Can they trivially do it elsewhere free? | Easy to replace | Uniquely ours |

**Reading the score**

| If… | Disposition | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| High Growth + high Frequency | **Free** | Gating starves the loop — this is your marketing budget. |
| Low Growth + high WTP + low Cost | **Premium** | Pure upside — bundle into Pro. |
| High Growth *but* high WTP at the extreme | **Usage-limited** | Free to a threshold (3 capsules, 20 photos), paid beyond. Everyone tastes it; heavy users pay. |
| High marginal Cost, bursty need | **Credit / one-time** | Video minutes, AI recaps, event capacity — sell as consumable/Event Pass so cost scales with revenue. |
| Loved core feature, mixed signals | **Partial** | Free at a standard allowance; premium unlocks depth (bulk export, unlimited members). |

> **Worked example — Video:** Growth 2 · WTP 4 · Cost 5 · Frequency 3 · Substitutability 3 → high cost + high WTP + low growth-risk = **Premium quota** (free tier photos-only or a tiny allowance; Pro unlocks it).
> **Being invited to a capsule:** Growth 5 · WTP 1 · Frequency 5 → unambiguous **Free forever**.

## 06 · Monetization trends (2025–26)

- **Subscription fatigue is real.** 62% of consumers report it; average US consumer holds 6.7 active subs. Trial-start resistance rises for sub-only apps. → A one-time purchase option (Event Pass) is a genuine competitive advantage.
- **Hybrid models & credits.** Fastest-growing structure mixes subscription + consumables + usage tiers; credits/coins booming, especially to meter AI cost. → Price expensive, bursty things (AI recaps, extra event capacity) as credits.
- **AI as a price ceiling.** Duolingo Max ($29.99) uses AI to justify a *higher* tier, not replace the base. → A "Capsule Recap" auto-highlight film is a future top-tier anchor — additive, never a wall.
- **Seasonal & occasion offers.** Capsule's calendar is built-in: wedding season, graduation, holidays, New Year. → Run Event Pass promos on the occasions themselves.
- **Referral rewards.** Give-to-get loops turn users into acquisition. → Reward invites that convert with a free Pro month or bonus storage.
- **Creator / host monetization (later).** A B2B/host tier for wedding photographers, coaches, schools who run capsules for clients.

Microtransactions and bundles round it out: cosmetic packs (theme bundles) are proven impulse buys, and bundling Pro + an Event Pass credit at a slight discount is a clean upsell once both exist.

## 07 · Growth interlock

| Lever | How monetization must behave |
|---|---|
| Onboarding | No paywall before first value. First session = create or join one capsule, feel the anticipation. Trial/paywall appears *after* the first unlock. 80–90% of trials start Day 0 — the "aha" and the ask live here. |
| Retention | The habit (countdowns, adding photos, reveal day) is free. Paid features deepen engagement (recurring groups) rather than gating it. |
| Virality | **Sacred.** Invites, joins, QR, share links free and unlimited on the free tier. Every gate stress-tested against "does this cost me an invite?" |
| Referrals | Actively rewarded: invite-that-converts → free Pro time / storage. Turns the growth loop into a paid-conversion funnel. |
| DAU | Notifications, reminders, reactions, awards — all free. Daily engagement is never walled; it earns the right to upsell. |
| UGC | Contributing photos/video is free for all members. Premium touches capacity and format (video length), not the ability to add or the quality of what you add — every photo is resized to 1920px for everyone. |
| Network effects | Each free member makes every capsule better and seeds new capsules. The free tier compounds; the paid tier harvests. Never invert this. |

## 08 · Recommendations for Capsule

**Free forever.** Join/view/contribute/react/vote in any capsule; the unlock reveal; **up to 3 active hosted capsules**; photos; **~10 members** and **~20 photos** per free capsule; invite links, QR, friends, surprise mode, basic awards, proximity check-in, reminders, one accent color. A new user can run their whole first event without paying, and invite everyone they know.

**What hosts will actually pay for (Capsule Pro).**

- **Video** — obvious cost story, obvious value; the single clearest gate.
- **Unlimited capsules & higher member/photo caps** — the engaged host bumps these constantly.
- **Recurring groups** — your most defensible upsell; automation nobody else offers.
- **ZIP export — download your whole capsule** — "let me keep every photo, in one tap" is an easy yes.
- **Cosmetics** — premium palettes, themes, a badge. High margin, brand-appropriate.

**The differentiated bet: Event Passes.** You already have QR joins, groups, proximity unlock, and time-locking — the exact primitives a **wedding, reunion, or team-season capsule** needs. Package that as a **one-time Event Pass**: unlimited guests, unlimited photos, custom unlock date, QR sharing, and (higher tier) video + branded title card + ZIP download. A wedding couple who'd never subscribe will pay **$25–$50 once** without blinking — price sensitivity collapses around the occasion, it dodges subscription fatigue, and *no competitor owns this flow.* Expect it to rival or beat subscription revenue in year one.

**How to not feel "pay-to-use."**

- Never interrupt a cold open with a paywall. Gates appear only when a host *reaches* a limit mid-action.
- Every gate has a plain-language reason ("Videos use more storage — Pro unlocks them"). Cost-justified gates feel fair; arbitrary ones feel greedy.
- Guests never see a paywall, ever. Most people experience Capsule entirely for free — that's the point.
- Frame Pro around *the memories*, not features — "keep every capsule forever, and take it with you."

**When to introduce it.** Not during beta — instrument first, learn where free users hit walls, build goodwill. Introduce **Pro + Lifetime at public launch**. Add the **Event Pass and Premium tier ~3–6 months in**, and the **web-checkout discount + B2B host tier** once you have MRR to protect.

### Risks & tradeoffs

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Free tier too generous → nobody converts | Caps (3 capsules / 10 members / 20 photos / photos-only) tuned so a *real* host bumps them but a casual user never does. Instrument in beta and tighten with data, not guesses. |
| Free tier too stingy → virality dies | The bigger danger for a social app. Keep all invite/join/contribute paths unlimited and free; only *hosting volume* and *format* (e.g. video length) are gated. |
| Lifetime cannibalizes recurring revenue | Strict 90-day window, then killed permanently; treat as launch marketing spend, not a product. |
| Subscription fatigue suppresses Pro | The Event Pass is the hedge — a one-time purchase that captures fatigued, occasion-driven buyers subscriptions can't. |
| Apple's 30% erodes thin consumer margins | Web checkout at a visible discount once at scale; keep IAP for convenience, steer price-sensitive users to web. |
| Cosmetic-heavy monetization feels shallow | Anchor Pro on *substance* (video, unlimited capsules, export, recurring groups); cosmetics are the topping, not the meal. |
